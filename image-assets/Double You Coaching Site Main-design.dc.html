Our Work

Our centers around the world

A large number of young women in the countryside of Rwanda are at risk of poverty, exploitation, and high-risk situations. Our local DYC center supports and equips them to escape poverty, secure reliable incomes, and provide for their families — through vocational skills training, mental health, and business start-up support.

90%
of graduates become self-reliant — employed or self-employed — able to support themselves and their families.
Sustainable Impact

Our training program leads to over 90% of graduates being employed or self-employed, meeting basic needs like housing, food, and education. Many go on to employ others, pay for siblings’ schooling, and improve living conditions for their families.

About Rwanda

One of the world’s most densely populated countries, divided by great peaks rising to 3,000m that run north to south. Nearly every piece of land is cultivated, and the Virunga volcanoes rise steeply from Lake Kivu into a striking, mountainous landscape.

Learn Kinyarwanda

Kinyarwanda is Rwanda’s national language, and most Rwandans are thrilled when visitors attempt even a little.

Amakuru? How are you? · Ni meza I’m fine · Murakoze Thank you · Murabeho Goodbye
Fun Facts

A traditional Rwandan instrument is the inanga — a wooden zither used to accompany the recitation of stories and poems, still played today. Musician Sophie Nzayisenga is considered its national icon.
